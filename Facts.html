This page is dedicated to spread facts about least likely animals to be recommended for adoption by shelters

PITBULLS -Did you know that “Pit Bull” is actually a catchall term that refers to multiple breeds? This includes the American Pit Bull terrier, American bulldog, American Staffordshire terrier, Staffordshire bull terrier and the English bull terrier. Then there are mixed breed dogs with similar characteristics that are labeled as such. This breed of dog is known for their strength, high intelligence, and affectionate nature with is normally taken as danger due to the size of this particullar breed. Because pit bull terriers have become so popular, some people are overbreeding them. Others neglect to have their dogs spayed or neutered or they can’t afford the surgery, resulting in unwanted litters. These two factors have led to an influx of pit bull terrier-like dogs in shelters. When there are a lot of the same types of pets available for adoption, people can easily get overwhelmed when trying to choose one to bring home, and they might end up leaving the shelter without a pet. Those dogs never get to realize their full potential as companions, therapy dogs, service animals, and even drug- or bomb-sniffing dogs. Best Friends’ no-kill mission means righting the misconceptions these dogs face — because that can mean the difference between good dogs going on to good homes or quietly dying alone in a shelter somewhere.

GERMAN SHEPARDS- German Shepherd is a dog developed in the late 19th and early 20th centuries to herd sheep. Their intelligence and trainability made them perfect for this task, and they quickly became popular among farmers and ranchers. Today, German Shepherds are still used for herding and various other tasks, such as search and rescue, protection, and therapy. This breed of dog is known for their versatility, loyalty, and protective nature. with such a protective nature this breed of dog tends to be highly guarded around strange enviroments and individuals . German Shepherds are energetic, athletic dogs who benefit from living with an owner who is happy to go on long walks and spend time playing lots of games with them, with the tendency for resource guarding , food guarding and play biting . if not properly trained .

BLACK CATS - Black is one of the most common feline color widely known between different feline breeds. Black cats are widely known for their connection to Halloween, witchcraft, and misfortune. However, despite their reputation, they are unique felines that are thought to bring good luck in some countries, and they have a rich history dating back to the Middle Ages. Some people went so far as to believe that black cats were cohorts to witches or even witches who had taken on another form. This widespread superstition resulted in the horrific mass killing of black cats—and sometimes even their owner. Unfortunately, black cats are often the last to be adopted from shelters due to lingering superstitions . These cats are genetically dominant, typically healthy, and considered good luck in many cultures In Japan, for example, single women who own black cats are believed to attract more suitors. In Great Britain’s English Midlands, a black cat is the ideal wedding gift; they’re believed to bring good luck and happiness to the bride. Black cats are naturally sweet-natured, loyal, and energetic, they are, despite the myths, affectionate house companions.
